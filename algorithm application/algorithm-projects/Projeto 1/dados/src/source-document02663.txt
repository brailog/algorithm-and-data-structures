Long known for its reputation as a leading research university, Indiana University has been named the "College of the Year Among Research Universities" by . Founded in 1820, Indiana University is the oldest university west of the Appalachian Mountains. Initially chartered as the State Seminary, the school became Indiana College in 1828, and finally Indiana University in 1838. The Bloomington campus has a student body of almost 38,000 and is located on 1,931 acres (781 hectares) just to the northeast of the downtown area.
