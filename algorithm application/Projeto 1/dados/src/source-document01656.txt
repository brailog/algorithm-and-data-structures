 
  
Dotted with a flaky biscuit pastry, this cobbler is a perfect addition to a fall brunch. For a rustic touch bake the cobbler in a cast-iron skillet. Cool the sizzling apples with a dollop of freshly whipped sweetened heavy cream. Mmmmmm.Home Style Apple Cobbler
email to a friend
Prep Time: 60 minutes, Serves 6 5 cups assorted organic apples, peeled, cored and sliced (Try two or three of the following: Fuji, Gala, Jonagold, McIntosh and Granny Smith) 1/2 cup sugar 3 Tbps. organic unbleached flour 1 Tbps. cinnamon 1/4 tsp. sea salt 1 tsp. vanilla extract 1 Tbps. butter, softened 1/2 cup organic, unbleached flour, sifted 1/2 cup sugar 1/2 tsp. sea salt 1 tsp. almond extract 3 Tbps. butter, softened 1 egg 1/4 cup of milk Freshly whipped sweetened heavy cream Pre-heat oven to 375 degrees F. In a medium sized mixing bowl, combine apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, salt and vanilla. Spoon into a 9-inch circular pan. Dot apples with one tablespoon butter. Combine flour, sugar, salt, three tablespoons butter, almond extract, egg and milk. Beat with a spoon until smooth. Drop batter, in 8 evenly spaced dollops, onto the apples. Bake 30 to 35 minutes or until the crust is golden brown. Serve warm with freshly whipped, sweetened cream.
